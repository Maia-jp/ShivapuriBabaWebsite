# Founding Principles

This document is the cornerstone of the Shivapuri Baba website. Every decision —
editorial, technical, and design — must serve the four pillars below. When a
choice conflicts with a pillar, the pillar wins.

> **Purpose.** To preserve and share the life, teachings, and legacy of the
> mystic Shivapuri Baba in a way that is free, trustworthy, beautiful to read,
> and open to all.

> **Stewardship, not authorship.** This project is never about its authors or
> contributors — it is about **Shivapuri Baba's message**. Those who build it are
> stewards of that message, not its subjects. No section, credit, or design
> choice should center the people behind the site over the teaching it carries.

---

## 1. Non-Profit, Always

This project exists to serve readers, not to generate revenue.

- It will **never** be monetized — no ads, no paywalls, no trackers, no selling
  of user data.
- It will never be acquired, rebranded, or repurposed for commercial gain.
- Any costs (hosting, domains) are kept minimal and, where possible, covered by
  voluntary contribution rather than commercialization.

**Why:** Trust in a source about a spiritual figure cannot coexist with a profit
motive that rewards engagement over accuracy.

## 2. Academic Rigor Over Rumor

The content is held to a scholarly standard.

- Every substantive claim is **sourced and referenced**. Prefer primary sources,
  then peer-reviewed and reputable secondary works.
- Clearly distinguish **fact**, **interpretation**, and **tradition/legend**.
  Never present unverified stories as established history.
- Cite openly so readers can follow the trail and judge for themselves.
- When evidence is thin or contested, say so plainly rather than filling the gap
  with speculation.

**Why:** The goal is to inform, not to evangelize or mythologize. Readers deserve
references, not rumors.

## 3. The Best Possible Reading Experience

Design exists to serve the reader and the text — nothing else.

- **Fast and reliable** above all: minimal JavaScript, optimized assets, works on
  slow connections and modest devices.
- **Readability first:** generous typography, comfortable line length, strong
  contrast, and a calm, distraction-free layout.
- **Accessible** to everyone (WCAG-minded): semantic markup, keyboard navigation,
  screen-reader support, and respect for reduced-motion preferences.
- **Resilient:** content remains usable even when scripts fail to load.

**Why:** A source of contemplative material should itself feel calm, clear, and
effortless to read.

## 4. Open Source

The project belongs to the community.

- All code (and, where rights permit, content) is **publicly available** under an
  open license.
- Development happens **in the open** — transparent history, public issues, and
  welcome contributions.
- Anyone may fork, audit, improve, or self-host the project.
- Built on open, standard technologies to avoid lock-in.

**Why:** Openness is the natural extension of a non-profit, trustworthy mission —
it invites scrutiny, collaboration, and longevity beyond any single maintainer.

---

*These pillars are deliberately stable. Proposals that touch them should be rare,
discussed openly, and recorded here.*
